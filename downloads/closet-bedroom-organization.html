üîÑ Montana's 4-Season Rotation System

Seasonal Clothing Management

The Rule: Only current season + transition pieces in active closet space

Winter Storage (December-March):

  • Heavy coats, snow pants, wool sweaters front and center
  • Summer dresses, shorts, swimwear stored in bins
  • Base layers and thermal underwear easily accessible
  • Winter boots at entry, summer shoes stored

Spring Transition (April-May):

  • Layer-friendly pieces take priority
  • Light jackets and cardigans accessible
  • Pack away heavy winter items as weather stabilizes
  • Bring out hiking boots and outdoor gear

Summer Access (June-August):

  • Shorts, t-shirts, sundresses in prime real estate
  • Swimming and outdoor recreation gear accessible
  • Winter coats stored in garment bags
  • Sandals and hiking shoes easily reached

Fall Preparation (September-November):

  • Layering pieces return to active rotation
  • Hunting gear and warm outdoor clothing accessible
  • Summer-only items begin migration to storage
  • Boots and closed-toe shoes move forward

Montana-Specific Shoe Storage

The Challenge: From snow boots to hiking boots to dress shoes - Montana lifestyles require lots of footwear

Solutions by Type:

  • Everyday Shoes: Shoe rack or over-door organizer for daily rotation
  • Seasonal Boots: Boot storage near entryway during active season

  • Dress Shoes: Individual boxes or clear shoe drawers
  • Work Boots: Mudroom or garage storage with cleaning station

üõèÔ∏è Bedroom Organization Beyond the Closet

Nightstand Organization

  1. Keep only essentials: lamp, alarm, water, current book
  2. Use drawer dividers for small items
  3. Charge station for electronics
  4. Small tray for jewelry or pocket items

Dresser Drawer System

  1. Top Drawer: Underwear, socks, organized with dividers
  2. Second Drawer: T-shirts, tank tops, folded uniformly
  3. Third Drawer: Pajamas, workout clothes, loungewear
  4. Bottom Drawer: Heavy items like jeans, sweaters

üì¶ Under-Bed Storage Solutions

Maximize Hidden Space

  • Seasonal Clothing: Vacuum-sealed bags for bulky items
  • Extra Bedding: Sheet sets, comforters, blankets
  • Holiday Decorations: Well-labeled containers
  • Memory Items: Photo albums, keepsakes in protective boxes

Montana Tip: Use cedar blocks or lavender sachets to protect stored items from moisture and pests.

üí° Montana-Specific Tips

Climate Considerations

  • Dry Air: Use cedar blocks instead of mothballs for natural protection
  • Temperature Swings: Layer-friendly organization system essential
  • Snow/Mud Season: Separate storage for dirty vs. clean outdoor gear
  • Limited Natural Light: Good closet lighting is crucial for color matching

Space Maximizing for Smaller Mountain Homes

  • Use vertical space with double-hang closet rods
  • Over-door organizers for accessories and small items
  • Under-bed storage for seasonal items
  • Multi-functional furniture (storage ottoman, bench with storage)
