How to Use These Labels

  1. Print: Use standard 8.5x11 paper or cardstock for durability
  2. Cut: Cut along borders to create individual labels
  3. Laminate (Optional): For frequently-handled or damp areas
  4. Attach: Use tape, adhesive, or label maker backing
  5. Customize: Write in specific items or locations as needed

Montana Tip: Laminate labels in mudrooms and kitchens to protect from moisture!

üéØ Pro Labeling Tips

  • Be Specific: "Winter Coats" is better than "Clothes"
  • Use Pictures: Add simple drawings for kids or visual learners
  • Consider Color Coding: Use different colored paper for different rooms
  • Place Strategically: Labels should be visible when containers are in use
  • Update Regularly: Replace labels when contents change
  • Make Them Removable: Use removable adhesive for flexible systems
